Financial Risk Analysis

Understanding and categorizing financial risk levels

High Risk

Individuals or transactions with a high probability of default or fraud.

  • Low credit score
  • High loan exposure
  • Unusual transaction behavior

Medium Risk

Moderate risk cases that require monitoring.

  • Average credit score
  • Balanced income-to-loan ratio
  • Occasional unusual activity

Low Risk

Financially stable individuals and transactions.

  • High credit score
  • Low loan burden
  • Regular transaction patterns

Risk Calculation Method

The system calculates risk using a combination of predefined rules and data-driven analysis. Key parameters include:

  • Age
  • Monthly Income
  • Loan Amount
  • Credit Score
  • Transaction Amount
  • Transaction Frequency and Timing

Based on these parameters, a risk score is generated and categorized into Low, Medium, or High risk.

Key Risk Insights

Credit Score Impact

Lower credit scores significantly increase financial risk and default probability.

Loan-to-Income Ratio

High loan amounts compared to income indicate repayment challenges.

Transaction Behavior

Unusual transaction timing and high-value transfers may indicate fraud.
